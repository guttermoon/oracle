Color philosophy

Borders are theme-tinted, not solid

A flat #04041C border on cream reads as too heavy. Tint instead: --card-border = 10–25% of text-color, --card-border-highlighted = 18–50%. Always 1px width.

Shadows are tinted, not pure black

Card shadows tint toward --text-color, never pure black. Modal is always 0 25px 50px -12px rgba(0,0,0,.5) regardless of theme.

Glass & blur — nav only

Blur (backdrop-filter: blur(10px)) is for nav and overlays only. Cards are opaque. --card-bg-glass is a solid color despite the legacy name. Modal backdrop is solid rgba(0,0,0,.8), never blurred.

No gradient anything

No gradient buttons. No bluish-purple gradient backgrounds. No generic vibe gradients. Buttons use flat solid accent fills only.

Moon page

The Moon (lunar phases) page uses atmospheric overlays (star tile patterns, particles, ink texture) but stays within the existing theme palette. Do not introduce jewel tones, amethyst, or gold accents. The moon orb itself is CSS-painted. No canvas script in the design system. Use a placeholder SVG for static mocks.

Three illustration registers

Starboard runs three distinct illustration registers. They are not interchangeable. Pick one per surface and stay there. Don't mix registers in one composition. Inked decoration with inked decoration. Board art with board art. Oracle with oracle.

Foundation

Background & texture

The aged-parchment texture is the default page background in every theme. A fixed SVG fractal-noise layer overlaid on the theme bg-color. Atmospheric overlays (star tile patterns, particles, ink texture) are Moon page only, staying within the existing theme palette. No flat color replacements.

Dark — fractal noise on #1B1F3B
Light — fractal noise on #FEFDF6
Warm — fractal noise on #F7F0DB
Parchment texture

.bg-parchment-texture — SVG feTurbulence fractal noise, position: fixed; attachment: fixed. Opacity 4–8%. Covers the whole viewport so textures are consistent when scrolling.

Decorative element opacity

Brand SVG/WebP elements always render at opacity 0.25–0.40 and float behind content. Never use as primary content. Dark-bg WebP variants work universally across all three themes.

Hero parallax

4 large elements + 2 background elements placed absolutely around the hero, shifted by mouse position (±20–40px). CSS only for reduced-motion fallback, elements simply sit statically.

No photography on brand surfaces

Marketing, onboarding, emails, and slides contain no photography. User-uploaded vision board photos are user data, not brand assets. Treat them as warm, sun-flared lifestyle imagery.

Steeped in lore — long-form writing

Blog, newsletter, social, and Instagram are edutainment. Every piece leaves the Sailor feeling enriched. A single surprising fact, told plainly, that opens into a wider story. No Such Thing as a Fish cadence: here is something interesting, and here is why it matters.

Maritime history & lore

Etymology of lanthorn. Why ships are called she. The origin of starboard, port, posh, the bitter end. Naval superstitions: no whistling on deck, never sail on a Friday.

Mythology

Sea gods: Poseidon, Manannán mac Lir, the kelpie. Constellations and navigation (Cassiopeia, Orion's belt). Norse navigational mythology. Selkies. The Morrigan as crow.

Science

Reticular activating system and why repetition rewires attention. Lunar tidal physics. Neuroplasticity and behaviour change. Celestial navigation: sextant geometry, why the North Star sits where it does.

Botanical & seasonal lore

Why the Strawberry Moon is named for a narrow harvest window. Full moon folklore. Solstices, equinoxes, cross-quarter days as markers for practice.

Folk practice

Tarot iconography. Oracle decks. Sailor-jerry tattoo meanings: swallows = home safely, anchor = grounded, hold fast = endurance. Victorian navigational charts, monk illumination.
